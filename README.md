# 🎮 Gaming Analytics Case Study: Player Funnel, Conversion & Retention Analysis

## 🧩 Overview
This project analyzes player behavior for a gaming company, focusing on understanding **conversion, retention, and engagement** patterns across the player journey.

The analysis maps the player funnel from **Registration → First Deposit → First Bet → Active in First 30 Days**, measures conversion rates at each stage, and identifies where the largest drop-offs occur. It also segments players based on engagement and deposit behaviors to uncover key profitability drivers.

---

## 📊 Objectives

1. **Funnel & Conversion**
   - Build the player funnel: *Registration → First Deposit → First Bet → Active in first 30 days.*
   - Calculate **conversion %** at each stage.
   - Identify stages with **highest drop-off** and analyze by acquisition channel or cohort.

2. **Retention & Engagement**
   - Count **days active** in the first 30 days after registration for each player.
   - Group players into activity cohorts (1–2 days, 3–5 days, 6–10 days, etc.).
   - Identify which cohort contributes the **most to total deposits** and why.
   - Measure **time gap** between first deposit and first bet:
     - Report mean, median, 75th percentile, and max values.
   - Discuss how short vs. long gaps affect engagement outcomes.

3. **Player Segmentation**
   - Identify **top 10% of players** by total deposit amount.
   - Calculate their **share of total deposits (%)** to assess concentration.
   - Visualize **first deposit amount distributions** using bins/buckets.
   - Discuss clustering patterns and what they imply about player profitability.

---

## 🧠 Key Insights

- The largest **drop-off** occurs between *Registration → First Deposit*, suggesting conversion bottlenecks in early onboarding.
- **Cohorts active for 6–10 days** in the first month contribute the **highest total deposits**, showing a strong link between early engagement and monetization.
- **Shorter gaps** between first deposit and first bet correlate with **higher retention** and long-term activity.
- **Top 10% of players** contribute around **70–80% of total deposits**, indicating a **highly skewed player base** typical in gaming/e-commerce.
- **Deposit clustering** observed around marketing bonus tiers (e.g., ₹500–₹1000), common in acquisition campaigns.
